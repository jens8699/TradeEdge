# TradeEdge launch plan — first 14 days

Built 2026-05-01 after the product hit "ready to take real money" status. Goal of the first 14 days is **first 10 paying customers**, not 100. Quality > virality. Each one teaches you what to fix before pouring on traffic.

---

## The hook (lead with this everywhere)

> **"I'm a prop trader. I got tired of logging into 4 different dashboards every morning just to know if I was up or down. So I built TradeEdge — one view of every account, every firm, every payout. Free tier, no card to start."**

Why this hook works:
- **Personal/credible** ("I'm a prop trader, I got tired") — opens like a person, not a brand. Posters on Reddit/Discord smell marketing copy from a mile away; this doesn't read like that.
- **Universal pain** (4 dashboards every morning) — every multi-firm trader nods. This is felt every single day.
- **Concrete what-it-does** (one view, every account / firm / payout) — clear value, no jargon.
- **Soft, no-friction CTA** (free, no card) — removes the buying decision from the first read.

### Adapted per channel

**Reddit post opening:**
> "I trade across multiple prop firms — TopStep, Apex, MyFundedFutures. Got sick of opening a dozen tabs every morning just to see whether I was up or down for the week. So I built TradeEdge: one dashboard for every account, every firm, every payout. Free tier, link in profile if anyone wants to try."

**Twitter/X (1 tweet):**
> "Prop traders: how many tabs do you open every morning to check your accounts? I built TradeEdge to make it one. Free tier, no card needed. [link]"

**Discord (casual drop):**
> "Hey — I'm a prop trader who got fed up logging into TopStep + Apex + FTMO separately every day. Built a dashboard that shows them all at once. Free tier if anyone wants to test it: [link]"

### Other angles for variety (use as follow-up content, not as the lead)

- **The ROI angle** — "Stop guessing if you're actually profitable at each prop firm. TradeEdge shows you net ROI per firm." (Strong second post once you have the audience.)
- **The drawdown angle** — "Get drawdown alerts before you breach the line." (Visceral fear hook for fence-sitters.)
- **AI briefs** — "AI market briefs every morning, no copy-paste." (For the AI-curious crowd.)

## ⚠️ Reddit reality check (read before week 1)

Reddit has karma + account-age gates. New accounts get auto-rejected in most subs that matter (r/Daytrading needs ~500 karma; r/Forex requires 30+ day account age). **Don't expect Reddit traction in week 1-2.**

**What to do instead:**
- **Create the Reddit account on day 1** of week 1
- Spend 15 min/day commenting helpfully on prop trading posts. NO self-promotion.
- By week 3, you'll have ~200-300 karma + 2 weeks of account age — clears most subs' bars
- Then post your story (draft below — write now, sit on it)

Why this works:

## YouTube demo — your single highest-leverage marketing artifact

Make a 2-3 min screencast in week 2. Trading is visual — text descriptions don't sell, demos do.

What it should show (in order):
1. (10 sec) "Hey, I'm Jens, prop trader. Built TradeEdge because I was tired of juggling N dashboards." — face camera optional but adds trust
2. (30 sec) Click through your real dashboard — show the multi-firm view, the per-firm ROI section, the trial banner. Narrate what each thing solves.
3. (60 sec) Click "Log a trade" → fill out the form → save → show it appears in History with the account badge
4. (30 sec) Show MarketBrief — click ✦ on a headline → AI brief generates
5. (15 sec) "Free tier no card — link below. Lemme know what you'd want next."

Don't over-polish. Loom or QuickTime + narrate while clicking. Authenticity > production value for trading audiences. Upload as unlisted YouTube link, embed in every Reddit/Discord post.

## Sustainable daily time budget (3-4 hr/day)

Don't spend 4 hr/day on cold outreach — you'll burn out by week 2. Mix it:

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 30 min | Community presence — Discord/Reddit comments/X engagement. Be helpful, no pitching. Compounds. |
| 30 min | Outreach — 5-10 personalized DMs/day, one Reddit comment that could become a post |

| 15-30 min | Talk to users 1:1 — DM signups, watch session recordings, "what made you sign up?" |

Total ~3.5 hours, sustainable for weeks.

## Week 2 — Wider net (one channel at a time)

**Goal:** first paying customer. Don't go wide — go deep on one channel that worked in week 1.

### Reddit playbook

The trick on Reddit is to **write a genuinely useful post that happens to mention your tool**, not "check out my new tool".

**Title idea:** "I tracked the actual ROI on my prop firm subscriptions for 6 months. Here's what I found." Body: real numbers, real accounts. At the end: "I built a small tool to track this if anyone wants it."

Subreddits ranked by usefulness:
1. **r/Daytrading** — biggest, strictest. Save for week 3 when you have testimonials.
2. **r/Forex** — friendlier. Good first target.
3. **r/PropFirms** — small but exact match. Post here first.
4. **r/Trading** — variable.

**Rules:**
- One post per week max across all subs
- Never post the link in the title — only in the body
- Reply to every single comment for the first 4 hours
- If post is removed, message mods politely. Don't repost.

### Discord playbook

Most prop firm Discords have a #tools, #journals, or #self-promotion channel. **Ask the mod first** — earns goodwill.

Template DM to a Discord mod:
> "Hey, I'm a [TopStep / FTMO] trader and I built a journal that does multi-firm tracking + ROI per firm. Would your community find that useful, or is it not the right fit? Happy to give all your members a free Pro month if you'd like to share it."

The "free Pro month for the whole community" is genuine reciprocity, not a stunt. Some mods will love it.

### Twitter/X playbook

10 personalized DMs/day. NOT a copy-paste pitch. Find someone tweeting about prop trading frustration:
> "Saw your tweet about [losing your TopStep account / FTMO drawdown / etc.]. I just built something that might help you avoid that next time. Free to try, no card. [link]. Let me know what you think."

5/day is fine if 10 feels like a lot. Quality > quantity.

## What to fix before any of this lands

Before you start sending real traffic, validate these on a fresh-incognito browser:

- [ ] Landing page loads in <2 sec
- [ ] Sign up → land on Dashboard → see the welcome card → click "Log first trade" → form opens
- [ ] Settings → Subscription → upgrade modal opens cleanly
- [ ] Stripe Checkout shows "$0 today, $19/mo starting [date]"
- [ ] Test card payment → Pro Active within 5 sec
- [ ] Manage subscription → opens Stripe portal
- [ ] Cancel via portal → still Pro until trial ends (no surprise charge)
- [ ] Read every word of the landing page out loud — fix anything awkward
- [ ] Trial banner shows correct days remaining
- [ ] Mobile view of landing + dashboard works

**This pre-launch checklist takes ~30 min on Sunday. Don't skip it.**

## Things NOT to do in week 1-2

- ❌ Run ads. You don't know your conversion rate yet — you'll burn money learning what a $20 CPA test would tell you.
- ❌ ProductHunt launch. Save for week 4 once you have 5+ users + a testimonial. Premature PH launch = wasted shot.
- ❌ Cold-email lists. Looks spammy, low conversion, can get domain blacklisted.
- ❌ Post the same thing to 10 subreddits same day. One ban kills the angle for weeks.
- ❌ Hire a "growth hacker" or buy services from Twitter strangers. Always a scam.

## Questions to answer by end of week 2

These are the answers you NEED to keep building intelligently:

1. What's the single biggest reason free users don't upgrade?
2. Which channel (Reddit / Discord / DMs / X) brought paying customers?
3. What feature did people ask for that we haven't built?
4. What's the most common confusion in the first 5 minutes of using the product?
5. What's your gut — does this need to be 10x better, or do you just need 10x more people seeing it?

Q5 is the most important question in the whole launch. Get an honest answer.

## Tools you'll want before launch (can defer until needed)

- **Analytics:** Plausible (free for your traffic level) or PostHog (free up to 1M events). Without analytics you're flying blind on conversion.

These are all on `tradeedge_post_launch_backlog.md`. Don't build them pre-launch — wait until you need them.
